Getting Here

For those flying in, Windsor International Airport (YQG) and Detroit Metro Airport (DTW) are your best bets. Both offer public transportation and private taxis into the city. Flying into Toronto Pearson Airport (YYZ) is also an option.

Windsor International
YQG

  • Small and ineffectual
  • Porter, Swoop, and AirCanada mainly
  • Good option for some, but limited

Detroit Metro
DTW

  • Big and busy, with lots of access
  • Major hub for Delta
  • For transportation into Windsor, consider taxis or RobertQ

Toronto Pearson
YYZ

  • Can be a nightmare, but lots of access
  • Major hub for Ontario
  • 3 to 4 hour drive from Pearson to Windsor. Train and shuttles available

Getting Around

If you want to get out and explore the city yourself, there are plenty of ways to get around.

Car

Windsor and Detroit are... not the most walkable places. If you want to explore the area you'll want a car.

That being said, the streets of Windsor and Detroit are easy to navigate as the roads line up on a grid and all hotels offer parking (free in some cases, valet and self-park, for a fee, in others).

Taxi

Hailing a cab typically isn't too hard in the city. If you don't have cash, all cabs are required to accept credit cards. Legally, cabs are not allowed to accept more passengers than there are seat belts in the car. That being said, it never hurts to ask.

For more info check out Veteran Cab or some other vendor.

Public Transit

Yeah, Windsor isn't known for caring much about public transit. One shining light is the Windsor-Detroit Tunnel Bus, a shuttle that takes you to-and-fro for under $15. No need to worry about driving or parking!

Uber & Lyft

Ride-share? Uber tends to be our app-of-choice, but Lyft also has good coverage as well.
